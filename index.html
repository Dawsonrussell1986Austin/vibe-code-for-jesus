About us

Why we’re called it was very good.

At the end of each day of creation, God looked at what He had made and called it good. But on the sixth day — when He stepped back and saw the whole of creation together — He didn’t just call it good. He called it very good. Genesis 1:31 is the moment a perfect God declares a perfect creation finished, whole, and ready for the world He made it for.

That’s the posture we want to give every builder who walks through our doors. You are not God — your work won’t be perfect. But you are made in His image, and you were built to build. Our job is to help you ship the thing in your head into the world with intention, with craft, and with the courage to finally call it done.

Every first‑time builder’s biggest enemy is the same lie: “It’s not ready yet.” it was very good. is the answer. It’s permission to launch. It reframes done as an act of faith, not settling — because the One who is perfect already declared the work of His image‑bearers a calling worth pursuing.

The biblical layer

A perfect God created a perfect world, stepped back, and declared it very good. We carry that same posture into the work of our hands — not because we’re perfect, but because we were made by Someone who is.

The emotional layer

You were built in His image, to build in His likeness. It was very good is launch energy straight from the first chapter of Scripture — the antidote to “not ready yet.”

Frequently asked questions

What exactly is “vibe coding”?+

Vibe coding is building real software by collaborating with an AI like Claude in plain English. You describe what you want, Claude writes the code, and you guide the process. No CS degree required — just clarity, taste, and a willingness to learn.

Do I need any coding experience?+

None. The challenge is designed so a complete beginner can launch a real project in a single 3‑hour session. If you’re already a developer, the advanced modules will still teach you patterns most pros haven’t discovered yet.

What do I need before I start?+

A laptop (Mac, Windows, or Linux), an internet connection, and about 3 focused hours of your time. We walk you through every install, account, and setup step in the modules — you don’t need to know any of it before April 14.

How does the money‑back guarantee work?+

If you complete the full course and don’t have a launched website, app, or business within 3 hours of finishing, just email us. We refund every dollar — no questions, no hoops.

Is this only for Christians?+

Anyone is welcome. The course is built by Christians, for Christians, and our community reflects that — but the technical skills work for anyone building anything.

What kinds of projects can I build?+

Landing pages, full websites, e‑commerce stores, web apps, mobile‑ready PWAs, internal tools, AI‑powered SaaS products, course platforms, church sites — if you can describe it, you can vibe code it.

Will this still work as Claude updates?+

Yes. The course is updated continuously as Claude Code evolves, and your purchase includes lifetime access to every update.

How is this different from a YouTube tutorial?+

Tutorials show you how to copy. The Challenge gives you a roadmap, a community, live Q&A, battle‑tested prompt templates, and a deadline — so you actually finish and ship.

When does the Challenge actually start?+
